Essential Pharmacy

Well has 760 pharmacies making them the third largest pharmacy chain in the UK.

Their service model is based chiefly on dispensing NHS prescriptions.

Well set a strategic goal to transform their pharmacies through service design. Working with a pilot store, success would be measured by efficiency and growth.

Discovery

I set up a Discovery phase to analyse the prescription process and find evidence that highlighted key problems within the service. I also did user research to challenge assumptions about customer segments and identify user needs.

  • I met with the NHS Business Service Authority and observed how prescriptions are processed
  • I visited the main warehouses to see how stock was managed using the latest robotic machines
  • I spent time shadowing colleagues in-store to empathise with their experience as key enablers in the service journey
  • I created service maps of the end-to-end service journeys

Findings included:

  • 13% of the pharmacy floor space was allocated to the dispensary, meanwhile dispensing prescriptions accounted for over 90% of the revenue
  • There were bottlenecks in the process of dispensing prescriptions, meaning that some customers were waiting for over 15 minutes
  • The average time to dispense a prescription was 7 minutes
  • Dispensed medication was often un-collected for weeks, with over 200 bags on the shelves at a time
  • When customers came to collect their medication it was difficult for staff to find their bag in the dispensary - they call this 'the pantomime'
  • 82% of prescriptions are free repeat customers
  • Popular medication regularly went out of stock so customers would go to another pharmacy

The discoveries formed hypotheses to test in the Alpha phase.

Alpha

We set up a cross-functional agile team to design, prototype and test hypotheses that could be delivered into a pilot store.

The team included a lean-consultant, delivery manager, developer, interaction designer, a pharmacist, and myself as a service designer and user researcher.

Service are co-produced

I started the Alpha phase by getting the team to carry out service safaris and observe all the aspects of a service eg. the service offering, the touchpoints, the channels, the products.

Then I ran co-design sketching workshops with the store team to learn about their value systems and how they act as enablers of the service.

Prescription collection service

Based on the user needs identified during discovery we defined a number of stages that formed our first digital prototype that would remind repeat prescription customers that their medication was ready to collect.

I worked with my team to create a number of service blueprints so that the prototype truly worked through the technology stack.

We prototyped a new end-to-end prescription collection service that we delivered as an MVP into the pharmacy store.

Once we created our MVP prototype we set up a “mock-shop” pharmacy to work with the store team to iterate on the design and observe how the service played out in real scenarios.

Actors who enable the service

I worked with a lean consultant to deliver a new one-piece-flow process that enabled the team in the dispensary to process prescriptions in two and a half minutes rather than seven.

Physical prototype

It was also clear that the physical environment wasn't working effectively for staff and wasn't a good experience for customers so we partnered with Your Studio who specialise in interior design.

All the designs we rationalised using observations from discovery research and our one-piece-flow process for dispensing.

Outcomes

We delivered a new service journey for the pilot pharmacy that included a new interior with custom made stations to enable more efficient dispensing. Dispensing time was reduced by an average of 5 minutes and accuracy increased. It was made possible to track bags of dispensed medication in the dispensary using our prototype and existing pharmacy hardware. The prescription collection service went into a private Beta phase and has real patients signed up to the trial. The successful project was featured in industry magazines and nominated for several awards.
